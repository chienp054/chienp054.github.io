Capstone Project — EV Adoption

Our master’s capstone examined how charging-station availability drives electric-vehicle (EV) ownership across California ZIP codes.

  • Built regression, decision-tree, and random-forest models to predict EV penetration.
  • Identified the charger-per-capita threshold where marginal adoption gains flatten.
  • Segmented regions by income to recommend targeted charger incentives.

Map 1 Insight: EV adoption is higher in ZIP codes with higher income quartiles, suggesting targeted subsidies could boost adoption in lower-income areas.

Map 2 Insight: Clustering ZIPs by infrastructure and demographic data reveals underserved regions, enabling strategic charger deployment.

Map 3 Insight: High-adoption ZIPs cluster around metro areas, affirming urban focus for charger investment, but also indicating potential in secondary markets.

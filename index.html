Capstone Project — EV Adoption

Our master’s capstone examined how charging‑station availability drives electric‑vehicle (EV) ownership across California ZIP codes. We analyzed socioeconomic and infrastructure factors using regression, tree‑based, and deep‑learning models, plus GIS visualizations.

  • Identified the charger‑per‑capita threshold where marginal adoption gains flatten (~1.5 per 1k people).
  • Segmented income quartiles to highlight equity gaps and prioritize incentive programs.
  • Ranked high‑potential ZIPs to guide charger roll‑out strategy.

Finding 1: EV ownership clusters in Q4 (highest‑income) ZIPs.
Actionable insight → Target Q1–Q2 areas with rebates and workplace charging to accelerate equitable adoption.

Finding 2: K‑means clustering exposes four distinct regional EV segments.
Actionable insight → Craft region‑specific incentives (e.g., rural fast‑chargers vs. urban curbside) instead of a one‑size‑fits‑all approach.

Finding 3: A handful of metro ZIPs dominate current EV registrations.
Actionable insight → Redirect new charger investments toward underserved ZIP codes to maximize incremental gains.

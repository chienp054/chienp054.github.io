Capstone Project — EV Adoption

Our master’s capstone examined how charging‑station availability drives electric‑vehicle (EV) ownership across California ZIP codes. We analyzed how socioeconomic and infrastructure factors affect EV uptake using data science and GIS tools.

  • Built regression, decision‑tree, and random‑forest models to predict EV penetration.
  • Identified the charger‑per‑capita threshold where marginal adoption gains flatten.
  • Segmented regions by income to recommend targeted charger incentives.

Finding: Higher EV ownership is clustered in Q4 income areas. Insight: Incentives should target lower-income quartiles to promote equitable EV adoption. This map highlights the correlation between affluence and early EV penetration.

Finding: K-means clustering revealed clear geographic segments. Insight: Region-specific policies can be crafted to address localized EV adoption barriers. Distinct regional patterns emerged, suggesting demographic-based infrastructure needs.

Finding: A few metro ZIP codes dominate EV registration. Insight: Saturated areas suggest infrastructure growth should focus on underserved regions. These hot spots show where current investments are concentrated and where expansion is needed.
